A Culinary Melting Pot

Gastronomy

Where Malay, Chinese, and Indian flavors dance together on a single plate.

Nasi Lemak

Considered the national dish of Malaysia, Nasi Lemak is a fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaf. It is traditionally served with a spicy sambal, fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, and cucumber.

Char Kway Teow

A beloved stir-fried noodle dish cooked over intense heat (wok hei) with flat rice noodles, prawns, cockles, Chinese lap cheong (sausage), egg, bean sprouts, and chives in a mix of soy sauce and chili paste.

Laksa

A spicy, rich noodle soup that varies wildly by region. Penang's Asam Laksa features a sour, tamarind-based fish broth, while Sarawak Laksa blends sambal belacan and coconut milk for a deeply complex, earthy flavor profile.

Roti Canai

An Indian-influenced flatbread dish that is a staple of Malaysian breakfasts and late-night suppers (mamak). The dough is repeatedly kneaded, flattened, oiled, and folded to create a beautifully fluffy, flaky bread served with dhal or fiery curries.

Rendang

A rich and tender coconut beef stew which is explosively flavorful. Prepared with a complex spice paste (pemasak) including ginger, galangal, turmeric, lemongrass, and chilies, it is slow-cooked for hours until the liquids evaporate, caramelizing the meat.

Hainanese Chicken Rice

A dish of Chinese origin that has become a defining culinary staple of Malaysia. It pairs gently poached (or roasted) chicken with rice cooked in chicken broth and fat, served alongside a sharp chili-garlic dip and dark soy sauce.

Cendol

The ultimate icy, sweet relief from Malaysia's tropical heat. A bowl of finely shaved ice is soaked in sweet palm sugar syrup (gula melaka) and coconut milk, topped with green rice flour jelly worms, and red beans.

Nasi Kandar

A popular northern Malaysian dish originally from Penang. It is a hearty meal consisting of a mountain of steamed rice accompanied by a variety of curries, side dishes, and meats that are famously "flooded" (banjir) with mixed gravy.

Mee Goreng

A ubiquitous, comforting street food dish of fried yellow noodles. Often prepared "Mamak" style, it's tossed in a massive wok with sambal, dark soy sauce, potatoes, fried tofu, bean sprouts, squid, and a slice of lime to cut through the richness.
