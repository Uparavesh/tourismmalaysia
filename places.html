Footprints of the Past

Living Heritage

Walk through the majestic natural temples and colonial streets of a nation layered in history.

Batu Caves

A limestone hill comprising of iconic caves and temples situated outside of Kuala Lumpur. It takes its name from the Sungai Batu (Stone River). The majestic golden statue of Lord Murugan stands guard at the base of the 272 vibrant, rainbow-painted steps leading to the main cave.

Historic Malacca City

Wander through this UNESCO World Heritage site and trace the centuries of influence from the Malay Sultanate, the Portuguese, the Dutch, and the British. The red walls of the Stadthuys remain centerpieces of this living museum.

Jonker Street, Malacca

The bustling heart of Chinatown in Malacca. Renowned for its lively weekend night market, Jonker Walk is a vibrant corridor of antique shops, historic shophouses, and incredible local Nyonya delicacies that truly come alive after sunset.

A Famosa Fort

Among the oldest surviving European architectural remains in Southeast Asia, the Porta de Santiago is a solitary gate that is all that remains of the once-mighty Portuguese fortress constructed in Malacca in 1511.

Thean Hou Temple

A striking syncratism of Buddhism, Taoism, and Confucianism, this six-tiered temple rests on a hill in Kuala Lumpur. It's renowned for its stunning traditional architecture, ornate dragons, and the thousands of red paper lanterns strung across its courtyard.

George Town Heritage Area

The colourful, multicultural capital of Penang island. Its core is a beautifully preserved UNESCO World Heritage site known for its unique British colonial buildings alongside Chinese shophouses, mosques, and interactive street art that lines the narrow alleys.

Kek Lok Si Temple

The largest Buddhist temple in Malaysia, situated in Penang. A mesmerizing complex featuring millions of magnificent statues of Buddha, countless pagodas, and the towering 30-metre-tall bronze statue of Guanyin, the Goddess of Mercy.

Sarawak Cultural Village

Dubbed a 'living museum' at the foothills of Mount Santubong. Here, you can explore authentic replica buildings representing every major ethnic group in Sarawak and experience their traditional dances, crafts, and lifestyles first-hand.

Mari Mari Cultural Village

Located deep in the countryside near Kota Kinabalu, Sabah. It offers an incredible, immersive glimpse into the indigenous culture and traditions of Borneo's fascinating tribes, from the bamboo houses of the Murut to the longhouses of the Rungus.
